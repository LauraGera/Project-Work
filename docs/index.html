Cartes-Guides Campbell - La Bretagne

A glimpse into the dawn of French tourism

Around the turn of the (20th) century, Francis Campbell, about whom not much information is available to us, created the Campbell Maps and Guides (Cartes-Guides Campbell) which he distributed himself before entrusting them to the printing house Blondel la Rougery in Paris after 1906. This project deals with one of the early editions, perhaps one of the last before the takeover by Blondel la Rougery, as Francis Campbell is still listed on the cover as editor.


The Campbell maps were joined by a small touristic guide with explanations and recommendations about all the cities and places worth visiting in a certain region. It is this document that was digitised as part of the present project.
